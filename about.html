Mineral Exploration Across the Arabian Nubian Shield

Ubuntu Geoscience: your technical bridge to discovery in the ANS and beyond

Ubuntu GeoScience was founded by MT Menghisteab, an exploration geologist with close to 20 years of field geology experience in mineral exploration and project leadership across the ANS in both Eritrea, Sudan and West Africa. We specialise in the Arabian‑Nubian Shield mineral deposits studying the largest juvenile Neoproterozoic crust in the world.

Guided by the Ubuntu philosophy – “Integration of diverse data and field work. Our approach is field‑first and data‑driven, ensuring every decision is anchored on field observations supported by geospatial analysis of data.

We deliver practical, field‑grounded geological expertise that turns prospects into discoveries and advances projects toward development. Whether you need structural mapping, target generation, or exploration management, Ubuntu GeoScience acts as an extension of your team.

Key Experience Highlights

  • Key contributor to the discovery of the Wadi Dom prospect (Galat Sufur, Sudan) – a significant orogenic gold discovery.
  • Extensive gold exploration across the Nubian Shield: Ariab, Gabgaba, Dongola corridors.
  • Technical role in the Bisha VMS district (Eritrea) – from exploration drilling to resource estimation.
  • Structural mapping and core logging of the Daero Paulos Porphyry Gold System, Asmara Belt.
  • Artisanal and small‑scale gold mining (ASGM) assessment in Sierra Leone.
