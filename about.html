Mineral Exploration Across the Arabian Nubian Shield

Technical geology, structural interpretation & exploration targeting

Ubuntu Geoscience is a mineral exploration consultancy focused on structurally controlled ore systems across the Arabian Nubian Shield and West Africa. The work integrates field geology, structural interpretation, geochemistry, and GIS-based targeting to support exploration decision-making from reconnaissance through to resource definition.

The approach is field-driven and data-integrated, ensuring that all interpretations are grounded in geological observation and validated through spatial and geochemical datasets. The consultancy operates as a technical extension of exploration teams, supporting both early-stage and advanced mineral projects.

Geological Focus

  • Orogenic gold systems (Arabian Nubian Shield belts)
  • Volcanogenic massive sulphide (VMS) deposits
  • Porphyry-related gold systems
  • Structural geology and shear zone-hosted mineralisation
  • Greenfield and brownfield exploration targeting

Technical Experience

Close to 20 years of field geology and exploration experience across Eritrea, Sudan, and West Africa, working within major Neoproterozoic terranes of the Arabian Nubian Shield and associated mineral belts.

Key Project Contributions

  • Galat Sufur South (GSS), Sudan — Structural interpretation and geochemical targeting within orogenic gold systems of the Ariab–Gabgaba belt.
  • Wadi Dom Prospect, Sudan — Chip sampling across alteration zones and identification of geochemical gold anomalies within shear-hosted systems.
  • Bisha VMS District, Eritrea — Structural mapping, core logging, and exploration support within volcanogenic massive sulphide systems.
  • Daero Paulos Porphyry System — Structural interpretation and lithological mapping within the Asmara Belt.
  • ASGM Assessments, Sierra Leone — Field evaluation of artisanal mining zones and structural controls on gold mineralisation.

Ubuntu Geoscience delivers practical, field-grounded geological expertise that bridges early-stage exploration and resource development. The focus remains on converting geological understanding into actionable exploration targets and reducing uncertainty in decision-making.
